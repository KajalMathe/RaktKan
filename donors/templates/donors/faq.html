Frequently Asked Questions

Who can donate blood?

Individuals aged 18-65, in good health, and weighing at least 50 kg are eligible to donate blood. It's important to be free from any infections and have a healthy lifestyle.

How often can I donate?

You can donate whole blood every 3 months. For other types of donation like platelets, the frequency may vary. Always consult with the medical staff before scheduling another donation.

What are the health benefits of donating blood?

Donating blood can improve cardiovascular health, reduce the risk of certain cancers, and promote the production of new blood cells. It’s also a rewarding feeling to help those in need!

Is it safe to donate blood?

Yes, donating blood is a safe process. All equipment used is sterile, and the process is supervised by trained professionals to ensure your health and safety.

Can I donate blood if I have a medical condition?

Certain medical conditions may affect your eligibility to donate blood. It’s best to consult with your doctor or blood bank staff before donating if you have any chronic health issues or are on medication.

How long does the donation process take?

The actual blood donation process usually takes around 10-15 minutes. However, the entire visit, including registration, health check, and recovery, may take about 30-45 minutes.
